# Loyalty Program

> Internal knowledge base note: This is a synthetic, airline-neutral customer support reference for a RAG chatbot. It is written in original wording and should be adapted to the operating airline's filed tariffs, fare rules, local laws, and live airport notices before production use.

## Purpose

This document covers enrollment, earning, status, redemptions, missing miles, partner activity, benefits, and account security.

## How Customer Support Should Use This Document

- Use this article to answer general policy questions before checking booking-specific fare rules.
- When a customer's itinerary, ticket condition, immigration status, or disruption record changes the answer, verify the live reservation before giving a final response.
- Explain policy outcomes in plain language and avoid promising exceptions that require airport, revenue management, or regulatory approval.
- For safety, security, legal, and government-entry topics, direct the customer to official authorities when the final decision is outside airline control.

## Loyalty Program Summary

A loyalty program rewards customers for flying, using partner services, and engaging with eligible products. Benefits typically include miles or points, tier status, priority services, baggage benefits, upgrades, and redemption options.

| Term | Meaning | Customer Question |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Miles or points | Reward currency earned from eligible activity | How many points will I earn? |
| Tier status | Recognition level based on qualifying activity | Why did my status change? |
| Qualifying activity | Flights or spending that count toward status | Does this ticket count? |
| Redemption | Using points for flights or services | Can I book an award ticket? |
| Partner earning | Points from partner airlines or companies | When will partner miles post? |

## Enrollment and Account Profile

Customers should maintain one loyalty account with accurate personal details.

- The account name should match travel documents to support mileage credit and benefits.
- Duplicate accounts can split miles and delay status recognition.
- Email and phone details should be current for security, statements, and offers.
- Family pooling or household accounts may be available in some programs.
- Members are responsible for protecting passwords, PINs, and one-time passcodes.

## Earning Miles or Points

Earning depends on airline, fare, booking class, ticket price, distance, status level, and partner rules.

- Some basic or promotional fares may earn reduced or zero points.
- Taxes, airport charges, and some surcharges may not earn points.
- Codeshare flights may earn according to marketing carrier or operating carrier rules.
- Points may post after travel is completed, not immediately after booking.
- Retroactive credit requests require boarding pass, ticket number, and flight details.

## Tier Status and Benefits

Tier status rewards frequent customers with service benefits. The exact benefits depend on route, airport, cabin, partner airline, and membership level.

- Common benefits include priority check-in, priority boarding, extra baggage, lounge access, seat selection, and bonus earning.
- Status qualification may use qualifying miles, qualifying segments, qualifying points, or eligible spend.
- Award tickets may or may not count toward status.
- Status benefits can be restricted on partner, charter, basic economy, or light fares.
- Status expiry and soft-landing rules vary by program.

## Redeeming Miles or Points

Members can use points for reward flights, upgrades, extra baggage, seat selection, partner services, or vouchers depending on program rules.

- Award seat availability is limited and can differ from paid-seat availability.
- Taxes, fees, and carrier charges may still be payable on reward tickets.
- Changes and cancellations of award tickets follow award fare rules.
- Points plus cash options may be available but may value points differently.
- Expired points usually cannot be used unless reinstatement is offered.

## Missing Miles and Retro Claims

A missing mileage claim is used when eligible points did not post after travel.

- Members should wait the normal posting period before submitting a claim.
- Claims require accurate flight number, date, ticket number, boarding pass, and booking class.
- Partner flights may take longer to validate.
- Tickets credited to another program usually cannot also earn in this program.
- Non-eligible fares, staff tickets, industry discounts, and some group tickets may be rejected.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why did I not earn miles for my flight?

The fare may be ineligible, the loyalty number may be missing, the flight may be credited to another program, or the normal posting window may not have passed.

### Can I transfer miles to another person?

Some programs allow transfers or family pooling with fees or restrictions. Others do not permit direct transfers.

### Do reward tickets include taxes?

Reward tickets often require separate payment of taxes, airport charges, and carrier-imposed fees.

### Can my status give me extra baggage?

Often yes, but the benefit depends on route, operating carrier, fare, and membership tier.

### Can I merge duplicate accounts?

Usually yes after identity verification, but activity and benefits may be reviewed before consolidation.
