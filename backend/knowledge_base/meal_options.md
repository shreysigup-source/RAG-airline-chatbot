# Meal Options

## Purpose

This document covers standard meals, special meals, religious meals, medical meals, allergy guidance, child meals, and meal disruption handling.

## How Customer Support Should Use This Document

- Use this article to answer general policy questions before checking booking-specific fare rules.
- When a customer's itinerary, ticket condition, immigration status, or disruption record changes the answer, verify the live reservation before giving a final response.
- Explain policy outcomes in plain language and avoid promising exceptions that require airport, revenue management, or regulatory approval.
- For safety, security, legal, and government-entry topics, direct the customer to official authorities when the final decision is outside airline control.

## Meal Options Summary

Meal service depends on route, cabin, flight length, departure time, aircraft, catering station, and special meal availability. Customers should request special meals before the cut-off time.

| Meal Type | Meaning | Support Note |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Standard meal | Default onboard meal for cabin and route | May not be selectable in advance |
| Special meal | Meal prepared for dietary, religious, medical, or age needs | Requires advance request |
| Pre-order meal | Optional selection before travel | Availability depends on cabin and route |
| Buy-on-board | Paid food or drink on selected flights | Stock is limited |
| Infant or child meal | Age-appropriate meal option | Must be requested where available |

## Special Meal Requests

Special meals help customers with dietary, religious, medical, or age-related needs. Availability is not guaranteed on every flight.

- Requests should be made before the airline's meal cut-off time, often 24 to 48 hours before departure.
- Late requests may not reach the catering provider.
- Special meals are usually linked to each flight segment, not just the booking as a whole.
- If the flight changes, the meal request may need to be re-added.
- Cabin crew can try to help onboard but cannot create a special meal that was not loaded.

## Religious and Cultural Meals

Airlines may offer meal categories such as vegetarian Hindu, Hindu non-vegetarian, Muslim, kosher, Jain, vegetarian oriental, or other regionally relevant options.

- Meal codes and definitions vary by airline and caterer.
- Kosher meals may require longer notice and may be served sealed.
- Jain meals usually avoid root vegetables and are prepared according to specific restrictions.
- Muslim meals avoid pork and alcohol ingredients but certification practices may vary.
- Customers should choose the closest available meal code and avoid assuming a custom menu can be built.

## Medical and Allergy-Related Meals

Medical meals may include diabetic, low-salt, low-fat, gluten-free, bland, or lactose-reduced options, depending on airline supply.

- Airlines generally cannot guarantee an allergen-free cabin environment.
- Customers with severe allergies should carry medication and consult a doctor before flying.
- Nut-free buffer zones or announcements may not be available on all airlines.
- Gluten-free meals reduce gluten ingredients but cross-contact risk may remain.
- Ingredient information can change by caterer, airport, and season.

## Infant and Child Meals

Infant and child meals are designed for younger travelers when available.

- Infant meals may include pureed or jar-style food but parents should carry enough familiar food for the journey.
- Child meals are usually suitable for children above infant age but may not meet every preference.
- Baby milk, formula, and food are subject to security screening rules.
- Cabin crew may help warm bottles where safe and practical, but exact temperature cannot be guaranteed.
- Parents should request meals for each child passenger before the deadline.

## Meal Changes, Cancellations, and Disruptions

Meal requests can be affected by flight changes, aircraft swaps, misconnections, delays, and catering substitutions.

- A changed flight may not automatically carry the original meal request.
- If catering fails, crew may offer available alternatives but choices can be limited.
- Paid meals that are not provided due to airline fault should be reviewed for refund.
- Special meals may be served before or separately from the main service.
- Support should document meal complaints with flight number, date, seat, meal type, and photos when available.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How late can I request a special meal?

Customers should request special meals before the airline deadline, commonly 24 to 48 hours before departure. Late requests may not be loaded.

### Can the airline guarantee a nut-free flight?

Usually no. Airlines can provide guidance and may offer limited assistance, but they cannot guarantee a completely allergen-free cabin.

### Do I need to request a child meal?

Yes when child meals are not automatically included. Request it for each eligible child and each flight segment.

### Will my meal request move to my new flight?

Not always. After a flight change, support should verify that the meal request is attached to the new segment.

### Can I bring my own food?

Usually yes for personal consumption, subject to security, customs, destination food rules, and onboard safety instructions.
