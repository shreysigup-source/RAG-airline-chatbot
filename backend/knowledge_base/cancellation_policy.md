# Cancellation Policy

## Purpose

This document explains voluntary and involuntary cancellation, no-show rules, partial cancellations, deadlines, communication, and operational handling.

## How Customer Support Should Use This Document

- Use this article to answer general policy questions before checking booking-specific fare rules.
- When a customer's itinerary, ticket condition, immigration status, or disruption record changes the answer, verify the live reservation before giving a final response.
- Explain policy outcomes in plain language and avoid promising exceptions that require airport, revenue management, or regulatory approval.
- For safety, security, legal, and government-entry topics, direct the customer to official authorities when the final decision is outside airline control.

## Cancellation Policy Summary

Cancellation rules determine whether a booking can be cancelled, what fees apply, whether the remaining value becomes a refund or credit, and how no-show status affects the ticket.

| Cancellation Type | Meaning | Typical Customer Need |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Voluntary cancellation | Customer chooses not to travel | Understand fee and refund or credit value |
| Involuntary cancellation | Airline cancels or disrupts service | Choose rebooking, refund, or care options |
| Same-day void | Eligible ticket cancelled soon after purchase | Reverse transaction where market rules allow |
| No-show | Customer misses check-in or boarding deadline | Know remaining ticket value |
| Partial cancellation | One passenger or one sector cancelled | Preserve remaining itinerary correctly |

## Before Cancelling a Booking

Support should confirm the customer understands the outcome before cancellation is completed.

- Verify passenger name, booking reference, ticket number, flight date, and contact details.
- Check whether the ticket is issued, held, exchanged, partially flown, or already checked in.
- Review fare rules for cancellation fee, no-show fee, refund value, travel credit eligibility, and deadline.
- Confirm whether ancillaries will be refunded, transferred, or forfeited.
- Warn the customer if cancellation is irreversible or if seats and special services may be released immediately.

## Voluntary Cancellation

A voluntary cancellation occurs when the customer decides not to travel for personal reasons, changed plans, price concerns, or preference changes.

- Cancellation fees are determined by fare rules and may vary before and after departure.
- If the customer cancels before the no-show deadline, more value may be preserved.
- Some fares permit cancellation for travel credit but not cash refund.
- Some promotional fares have no residual value after cancellation.
- Customers should receive a cancellation confirmation by email, SMS, app notification, or agency record where available.

## Involuntary Cancellation by Airline

An involuntary cancellation occurs when the airline cancels a flight or cannot carry the customer as booked.

- Customers may be eligible for rebooking on the next available service, alternate routing, refund, or travel credit.
- Care such as meals, hotel, or ground transport may depend on cause, delay length, jurisdiction, and ticket conditions.
- Compensation rules differ by country and may not apply to extraordinary circumstances.
- Support should avoid assigning blame until the disruption reason is confirmed.
- Case records should note whether the customer accepted rebooking or chose cancellation.

## No-Show Handling

A no-show occurs when a passenger does not check in, does not board, or does not cancel before the required deadline.

- No-show fees may be higher than advance cancellation fees.
- Downline or return flights may be cancelled automatically if the itinerary is not used in sequence.
- Taxes may remain refundable even when the fare is not.
- Customers who miss a flight due to airline delay on the same ticket should be handled as disrupted passengers, not voluntary no-shows.
- Agents should check status codes before advising on remaining value.

## Partial Passenger and Partial Itinerary Cancellation

Customers may need to cancel only one passenger, one segment, or one ancillary service.

- Split the booking when required before cancelling one passenger from a multi-passenger record.
- Cancelling one segment can affect fare construction and remaining-sector validity.
- Throwaway or out-of-sequence travel may invalidate later flights.
- Codeshare and interline tickets may require ticketing desk support before partial cancellation.
- Special services such as wheelchairs, meals, and seats should be reviewed after changes.

## Twenty-Four-Hour and Same-Day Cancellation Rules

Some markets require or airlines voluntarily provide a short cooling-off period after purchase. The rule depends on country of purchase, departure timing, channel, and fare type.

- A same-day void may cancel the original transaction rather than process a normal refund.
- Tickets bought close to departure may be excluded from cooling-off rules.
- Travel agency bookings may follow agency rules and settlement deadlines.
- Ancillary services purchased separately may not follow the same void rule as the ticket.
- Support should check market-specific policy before promising fee-free cancellation.

## Cancellation Communication

Clear communication prevents disputes and repeat contacts.

- State the cancellation fee, refund or credit value, expiration date, and next action.
- Explain whether the original seat, meal, baggage, insurance, or lounge purchase is affected.
- Send confirmation to the contact details in the booking.
- Advise customers to keep the confirmation until refund or credit is complete.
- For disrupted flights, explain choices without pressuring customers into one option.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I cancel only one person from a family booking?

Usually yes, but the booking may need to be split first so the remaining passengers keep their flights.

### Will my return flight be cancelled if I miss the outbound flight?

It may be. Many tickets require flights to be used in order, so customers should contact support before missing a sector.

### Can I cancel after checking in online?

Often the check-in status must be reversed first, and rules depend on timing, airport control, and fare conditions.

### What happens if the airline cancels my flight?

Support should offer available rebooking, refund, or credit options according to the disruption policy and local law.

### Is cancellation the same as refund?

No. Cancellation removes the travel booking. Refund determines what money or credit, if any, is returned afterward.
