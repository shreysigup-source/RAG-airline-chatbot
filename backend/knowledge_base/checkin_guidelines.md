# Check-in Guidelines

## Purpose

This document covers online check-in, airport check-in, document checks, baggage drop, boarding passes, security flow, and late-arrival handling.

## How Customer Support Should Use This Document

- Use this article to answer general policy questions before checking booking-specific fare rules.
- When a customer's itinerary, ticket condition, immigration status, or disruption record changes the answer, verify the live reservation before giving a final response.
- Explain policy outcomes in plain language and avoid promising exceptions that require airport, revenue management, or regulatory approval.
- For safety, security, legal, and government-entry topics, direct the customer to official authorities when the final decision is outside airline control.

## Check-in Guidelines Summary

Check-in confirms that a passenger intends to travel and allows the airline to issue a boarding pass, verify documents, accept baggage, and complete seat or service checks.

| Stage | Customer Goal | Support Note |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Online check-in | Get boarding pass before airport arrival | May be blocked by document or payment checks |
| Airport check-in | Receive assistance and drop bags | Deadline varies by route and airport |
| Bag drop | Deposit checked baggage after check-in | Requires baggage tag and ID verification |
| Security | Clear passenger and cabin baggage screening | Controlled by airport authority |
| Boarding | Enter aircraft before gate closure | Late arrival can result in denied boarding |

## Online and Mobile Check-in

Online check-in is available for many flights through the airline website or mobile app. Availability depends on route, operating carrier, passenger type, and system readiness.

- Online check-in commonly opens between 24 and 48 hours before departure, but some airlines open earlier.
- Boarding passes may be digital, printable, or airport-collection only.
- Customers with document checks, special assistance, pets, infants, or unpaid services may need airport counter verification.
- Passengers should review seat, baggage, contact details, and travel documents before completing check-in.
- A boarding pass does not override visa, passport, health, or security requirements.

## Airport Check-in Deadlines

Airport deadlines protect flight punctuality, document checks, baggage loading, and security processing.

- International flights normally require earlier airport arrival than domestic flights.
- Premium cabins and frequent flyers may have priority counters but must still meet deadlines.
- Bag drop deadlines can close before boarding pass issuance deadlines.
- Late customers may be refused travel even when the aircraft has not departed.
- During holidays, weather disruptions, or airport congestion, customers should arrive earlier than the minimum deadline.

## Travel Document Verification

Document verification may include passport, national ID, visa, residence permit, return ticket, vaccination proof, transit eligibility, and destination forms.

- The airline may deny boarding if documents appear invalid, incomplete, expired, damaged, or inconsistent with the itinerary.
- Names on travel documents should match the ticket according to airline and border authority tolerance.
- Some destinations require passport validity beyond the travel date.
- Transit airports may require documents even when the customer does not leave the airport.
- Final entry permission is decided by government authorities, not the airline.

## Boarding Passes

A boarding pass shows flight, gate, boarding group, seat, and security information. It may be issued digitally or printed.

- Some airports require printed boarding passes or counter-issued documents.
- Customers should keep boarding passes until baggage and loyalty credit issues are resolved.
- Gate changes can happen after boarding pass issuance, so customers should monitor screens and notifications.
- A seat shown on a boarding pass can change for operational, safety, aircraft, or family-seating reasons.
- Customers with standby or waitlist status may receive a seat only at the gate.

## Baggage Drop and Tagging

Checked baggage must be accepted before the bag-drop deadline and tagged to the correct destination.

- Customers should verify the destination airport code printed on each bag tag.
- Old tags and barcode stickers should be removed before travel.
- Oversize, special, and restricted items may require a separate counter.
- Self-service bag drop may require scanning a boarding pass and placing the bag correctly on the belt.
- Baggage accepted after normal deadlines may be refused or marked subject to space where policy allows.

## Security, Immigration, and Gate Arrival

After check-in, customers must complete airport-controlled processes and reach the gate before boarding closes.

- Security screening can include electronics removal, liquid checks, body scanners, and secondary inspection.
- International departures may require exit immigration or passport control.
- Boarding gates can be far from check-in, especially at large airports.
- Gate closure time is separate from departure time.
- Customers who arrive after gate closure may lose their seat and be treated under late-arrival or no-show rules.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Why can I not check in online?

Common reasons include document checks, operating-carrier restrictions, unpaid services, special assistance, infants, pets, or airport-only verification.

### Do I still need to go to the counter if I checked in online?

Yes if you have checked baggage, need document verification, require assistance, or the airport does not accept your digital boarding pass.

### How early should I arrive at the airport?

Arrive earlier for international flights, checked baggage, special assistance, peak periods, or unfamiliar airports. Always follow the deadline shown by the airline.

### Can my seat change after check-in?

Yes. Seats may change for aircraft swaps, safety, family seating, operational balancing, or disrupted-flight handling.

### What happens if I reach the gate late?

The airline may refuse boarding after gate closure, even if check-in was completed.
